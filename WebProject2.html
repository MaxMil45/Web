Particle physics is a branch of theoretical physics that delves into the fundamental constituents of matter and the forces governing their interactions. At its core, it seeks to unravel the intricacies of the subatomic realm, where particles exist on scales far smaller than the dimensions comprehensible to our everyday experiences. In the framework of the Standard Model, the foundation of particle physics, matter is composed of elementary particles categorized into two main classes: fermions and bosons. Fermions, such as quarks and leptons, form the building blocks of matter, while bosons, including the famous Higgs boson, mediate the fundamental forces that govern the behavior of these particles. Quantum field theory provides the mathematical framework to describe these particles and their interactions. Fields permeate space, and each type of particle is associated with a corresponding field. These fields are dynamic and subject to fluctuations, giving rise to the inherent uncertainty inherent in quantum physics.
